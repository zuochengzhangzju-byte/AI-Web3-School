# 2026-05-18
<!-- DAILY_CHECKIN_2026-05-18_START -->



I finished the first sharing session of the bootcamp: “_Essential Knowledge and Architectural Skills Web3 Developers Need in the AI Era._” （[https://x.com/aiweb3school/status/2056351391853981936?s=20](https://x.com/aiweb3school/status/2056351391853981936?s=20)）

Teacher TC started with three questions:

-   Do we still need to learn the fundamentals if we already have AI tools?
    
-   Are Web2 and Web3 actually different worlds?
    
-   And is security something that can be considered after the design phase?
    

We started from the classic Web2 payment model, then moved into Web3 architecture and flows. Seeing the difference in structure made me realize why Web3 requires a very different way of thinking when designing systems, permissions, and security.

I’m more of a strategy and operations person in the startup world, so honestly, I found myself needing to replay parts 2–3 times and still getting lost sometimes. But with AI helping me analyze the concepts afterward, I realized maybe the logic flow of engineers and marketers is just fundamentally different. Engineers often start from problems, risks, and failure cases, then derive principles from them, almost the opposite of how marketers usually think.

One thing I learned from this session is the importance of systems thinking and risk awareness. Systems thinking helps me understand how flows operate, where the risk points are, how permissions should be designed, and also gives me a better perspective when communicating with engineers.
